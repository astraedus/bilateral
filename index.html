How to use this
  1. Choose something mild. Think of something mildly stressful (3/10, not 10/10). Or a safe, calm memory if you just want to relax.
  2. Rate the distress 0-10 in your head. Don't write it down. Just notice the number.
  3. Press Start. Follow the dot with your eyes, or listen to the tones. Keep the thought loosely in mind. Don't force anything.
  4. When the set ends, pause. Take a breath. Notice: did anything shift? A new thought? A body sensation? An image?
  5. If distress went down, try another set with the same thought. If something new came up, follow that instead.
  6. Stop whenever you want. There is no wrong way to end.
This is not therapy.

For processing specific traumatic memories, work with a trained EMDR therapist. Do not use this to process your worst memories alone.

Safe for: general stress, mild anxiety, relaxation, winding down, grounding.

If you feel overwhelmed: stop, open your eyes, feel your feet on the floor, breathe slowly. You are safe.
